Welcome to Blockchain

Blockchain is a part of Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT). DLT is a broad range of technological methods of ledger structure, blockchain is one method. Blockchain simply put is data transactions recorded into a block of data that is chained across time. Bitcoin every ten minutes records a block, Ethereum records a block every 13 seconds or so, each blockchain can vary. This method of recording transactions ensures data integrity and transparency. Once it is recorded it cannot be changed, altered or deleted, this is often referred to as immutability. Therefore, blockchain is brilliant for anything that requires a transaction - such as cross-border payments, IP, real estate, healthcare records, gaming, and more - as you can verify the data easily.

There are permissioned blockchains, where only selected participants are allowed into the network and validate transactions. These tend to be centralised and these can be seen in enterprises and used in supply chain management. Permissionless blockchains are a great way for people to collaborate and engage as they are decentralised systems. There arenâ€™t permissions or approvals acquired to participate. As long as you have an internet connection you can begin to get involved in the network.

Each blockchain has a consensus mechanism, there are various methods to forming consensus. A consensus mechanism is the process on how a network determines which transactions are true and which are not. The decentralised network of computers constitutes the nodes. The mechanism runs through nodes of the network, these nodes say which transactions should be added to the chain and this helps form a consensus on the true state of the blockchain.

Because of the nature of a digital stamp providing verified data, cryptocurrencies have found a place on blockchain. They are currencies that utilise cryptographic methods and DLT to provide peer-to-peer interaction and transaction. Blockchains can allow the function of smart contracts. Smart contracts enable automated execution. Someone can develop a smart contract, put in predefined rules and conditions that once met the smart contract can execute and the transaction will be recorded on the blockchain.

This effective idea has led to dApps, which stands for decentralised apps. The core of the dApps function is in the coding of the smart contract and these dApps can provide a service that users interact with. They tend to have a tokenised economy and there has been a broad range of dApps produced since the cryptocurrency market has begun. There are social media dApps, dApps ffor exchanges, gaming and NFT marketplaces.

From 2008 to 2024, the market cap of cryptocurrencies has gone from zero to, in 2024, over $2tn. This is just the beginning.
