Web2 vs Web3

Revolutions in information speed up progress in society.

The Guttenburg press simplified mass production of books. Information became more accessible to those that can read, those that read tend to pass on the knowledge they consume.

The internet broadened the boundaries of communication as people on opposite sides of the globe can communicate and share, cultures can contact far flung regions they may never physically visit.

YouTube and podcasts have again initiated another step in information spreading at a great speed.

The next leg up in the revolution was done with decentralised network of information nodes.

The big revolution with blockchain is how it impacts the financial plumbing for moving assets. It is everything when it comes to administration as it can automate it and the Move programming language helps secure assets on-chain.

Let’s go through the stages of the World Wide Web.

Web1 - Read

Web1 was basic and read-only, it is known as the Static Web; the user could only consume the contents of the page, interact with basic forums and make orders on eBay and food delivery website. It was the first stepping stone to today, its era spanning around 1991 to 2004.

Examples of Web1

  • LiveJournal
  • Google
  • Ask Jeeves
  • Pizza Hut

Web2 - Write

Web2 is where things get more interactive, where people could write. People can upload videos, share content, comment. It is a place for collaboration, communication and participation, a hub of interaction and driven by the user.

Smartphone apps particularly assisted the growth of Web2. People can post content and earn money through YouTube, Instagram, Tik Tok and other similar websites.

Web2 has issues of centralisation and users’ data being siphoned off and sold, yet the user does not see a penny of that. Many times, without consent the data of users is tracked and sold off. Web2 apps attract then extract, some of these tech firms are taking the data uploaded by users and feeding it to their AI models.

The app stores can kick apps off, governments can censor apps, they can seize bank accounts.

One example, March 26, 2013, during the Eurozone crisis, the Cypriot government seized bank accounts with deposits over $100k. This helped Bitcoins first major bull run as people sought a currency that was not controlled by central banks.

Characteristics of Web2

  • Friendly end-user function
  • Participatory
  • Wikis
  • Database Management

Web3 - Own

As technology advances it impacts the possibilities and options when interacting with the internet. We now have blockchain, AI and IoT, which all contribute to Web3.

Blockchain has enhanced the data security and transaction transparency.

One example of what blockchain has done alongside the internet is change fundraising.

J.R Willet made the first ICO, which stands for Initial Coin Offering. In traditional finance (tradfi), there are barriers to fundraising. Legacy systems are slow because they have legacy software, rules, government oversight, old methods and bureaucracy to deal with.

Willet put up his Bitcoin wallet address in a forum along with the whitepaper to Mastercoin, those that wanted to invest could send funds to that wallet and they received the equivalent in the agreed price of Mastercoin.

This simple move with the technology of blockchain cuts out many intermediaries, therefore costs and it speeds up transactions. Remittances is an area greatly improved in Web3 as apps cannot take large fees for simply sending money back to families. Web2 relies on the legacy systems whereas Web3 can utilise tokens to send funds directly to the participant.

Most of the important software is not created in the central hub labs of big tech. Web3 has grown out of crypto-networks, people across the world composing new methods from techniques established by fellow enthusiasts.

The best tech does not come from a top down approach of bosses telling developers what to do. It comes from the intrigue, experiement, curiosity and natural drive of the developer. Anyone that creates is an artist and the best art is from authenticity.

Web3 is decentralised and permissionless and as it does not navigate through centralised server’s participants cannot be censored. Making it distributed and robust, for example, decentralised storage systems help provide a sanctuary for data, another method of resistance against censorship.

Characteristics of Web3

  • Decentralisation
  • Trustless
  • Permissionless
  • People own their data

Decentralised co-ordination is incentivised by the token economy.

Bitcoin is a peer-to-peer decentralised currency, when Ethereum became a working protocol it enabled decentralised apps. When Uniswap was created after receiving an Ethereum grant, it helped to greatly increase liquidity in the market and this saw the rise of decentralise finance (DeFi).

Participants can put their tokens into a liquidity pool, this helps facilitate trade on decentralise exchanges. The participants receive yield by putting tokens into the liquidity pool.

Those that host a node to help provide consensus on the network receive tokens.

Web3 helps the creator economy. The blockchain keeps your integrity, a creator can reach their audience directly, and patronage too can be direct.

Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs) to non-crypto natives may see NFTs as simply expensive JPEGs. That is misconstruing an example of NFT implementation. NFT in daily life will be more common than most think, a passport will be an NFT, dental records, health records, in video games.

This is because each NFT has a core code, an identifier that is unique to that NFT, the metadata is attached to the owner. This can be applied to real-estate, concert tickets, access to exclusive content, minting music, books, it can help charitable fundraisng and more. NFTs help track the provenance and integrity of the data.

As blockchain has become stronger in its application, traditional finance and decentralised crypto finance is converging and this will increase the fluidity of finance.

Features of the Token economy

  • Real-world Assets
  • Data sovereignty
  • Automation
  • Stablecoins
  • DAOs
  • NFTs
  • DEX
  • Defi

Oncoming

RWAs are worth $12bn as of mid-2024, this excludes the $175 billion stablecoin market.

As RWA’s come on-chain this will not see less boom-bust cycles as these assets have more stability than DeFi protocols based on pure crypto-economies. Private markets are worth around $15tn, the entire real estate market was worth $634.9tn at the start of 2024. If just a fraction of this comes on-chain it is more than that eclipses the crypto market cap.

A key reason the asset manager firms, family offices, private markets and such like utilise DLT is due to the efficiency of it.

Let’s take the example of a regulated fund in Luxembourg. There is data that is convoluted to get to. A management company, an appraiser, a depository, a custodian, an administrator, they may all be asking the same question to get particular data however to gain access to the information they each have to reinvent the wheel. They have to construct their own software, which is usually kept in-house and it goes through various processes.

It costs a lot of time and money.

The word revolution gets banded around a lot, yet, changing the plumbing of this to an immutable and efficient blockchain is revolutionary. It cost the world economy about 2% of its GDP transmitting finance, blockchain can free up that cost, imagine what that 2% can do circling back into the economy.

Crypto may come across as complex, however, it has had a rapid progression in smoothing transactions and onboarding participants. As crypto progresses it will become seamless and be taken for granted. Those that are a part of it are changing the world.

It is not just about the large asset firms coming to chain, blockchain has been helping the distant communities receive aid, receive remittances. It has helped the emancipate men and women as markets can engage in peer-to-peer systems, setting up community DAOs, opening up access to finance channels that can help projects thrive such as agriculture.

As the industry grows, it there are more jobs, more liquidity flowing through communities.
